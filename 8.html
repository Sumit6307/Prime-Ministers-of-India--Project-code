Prof. C. V. Chandrasekhar

A Legend of Bharatanatyam

I chosed to create a tribute page for Prof. C. V. Chandrasekhar. Because his path inspired me. He dedicate his life to dance and keeps the treasure of the ancient indian dance and give it further to his students. You will see his one pointed focusing on dance, in his biography. When you see him dancing (a video is added below), you can sense the pureness of his art. He himself, despite of all the honor he got (see Awards) remind humble. Born 1935, he is now 82. Still his movements are like a river flowing, his mimic got polished over the years of practicing.

Life

  • Born 22th May 1935 in Shimla, Himachal Pradesh
  • He is an academician dance scholar, composer and choreographer
  • He and his wife Jaya Chandrasekhar are one of the best known dancing couples of Bharata Natyam in India
  • During the 1970s and ’80s, they performed with their daughters Chitra and Manjari
  • He now runs his own dance institution, Nrityashree, in Chennai

Career

  • He trained at the internationally renowned Kalakshetra in Chennai
  • Teacher: Shrimati Sarada Hoffman, S. Sarda, Rukmini Devi Arundale, Karaikkal Saradamba, K.N. Dandayudha Pillai and Mylapore Gowri Amma
  • 1958 He start his career as dancing teacher at Vasant Kanya Mahavidyalaya, Varanasi
    • after that he impartet as teacher in different dancing schools
  • 1965 – 1979 he was lecture in dance Banaras Hindu University, Varanasi
  • He retired as Head of the Department of Dance and Dean of the Faculty of Performing Arts

Awards

He received many awards for his outstanding contribution to indian classical dance:

  • 1978 Uttar Pradesh Sangeet Natak Akademi Award
  • 1982 the Gujarat Sangeet Natak Akademi Award
  • 1993 the Sangeet Natak Akademi Award
  • 2003 the title of Kalaimamani bestowed by the Tamil Nadu Eyal Isai Nataka Manram
  • 2008 the Kalidas Samman
  • 2011 the Padma Bhushan
